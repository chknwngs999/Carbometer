Steps to improve your carbon footprint:

Eat less meat! A UN report states that livestock farming alone produces 18% of the worldwide greenhouse gas emissions. You can also substitute beef and pork, two of the largest CO2 emitting meats, with legumes and other plant based proteins. By reducing your meat intake, you are effectively decreasing your contribution towards one of the largest greenhouse gas emitting markets.

Eat locally grown items. Trucks, ships, and planes transporting the food you eat also emit greenhouse gases. Although transporting the food is unavoidable, you can still reduce your emissions by buying locally grown items, which would travel fewer miles and have fewer emissions as a result.
